De novo genome assembly is all about assembly programs, right? Well... The recent assembly-competeion papers already pointed out the value of trimming reads, and error-correcting them, see GAGE-B and Assemblathon. But there is much more to assembly than this.

With this post, I will list a few of the things one can (should?) do before, and after the actual process of performing the assembly. There are the following limitations:

  • I do no treat preparing samples, deciding on a strategy (library type, sequencing platform and type, etc.)
  • I will say nothing about the actual assembly programs and this post should be seen as independent of such programs
  • the approaches I use only make use of the read data available to you, and the assembly results (no optical maps, fosmids, RNA-Seq data etc)
  • No reference is needed for these approaches - which is the point of this whole post
  • This list is by no means complete, and tools listed are a selection of those availbe - which tools I list should not be seen as an endorsement - I just happen to know (of) them.
  • I repeat, no reference is needed
